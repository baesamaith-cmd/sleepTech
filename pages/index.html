CBT-I overview

Start with structure, not just sleep tips

CBT-I, or cognitive behavioral therapy for insomnia, is a structured behavioral approach that is commonly recommended before relying only on sleep medication. This app supports practice and tracking, not diagnosis.

Stimulus control

What to do when sleep feels stuck

  • Use bed mainly for sleep, not for scrolling, planning, or worrying.
  • If you are not drifting off after about 20 minutes, get out of bed for a brief quiet reset.
  • Return only when you feel sleepy again.
  • Keep your wake time as consistent as you reasonably can.
  • Remind yourself that calm repetition matters more than one perfect night.

Sleep restriction caution

Helpful for some people, not casual for everyone

Sleep restriction or sleep compression can feel harder before it feels better. Use extra caution and consider professional support if you have severe daytime sleepiness, driving risk, bipolar disorder, seizures, severe depression or anxiety, or possible sleep apnea.

This app is educational support only. It does not diagnose insomnia or tell you what is medically safe for you.

Quick action plans

I can’t fall asleep

  1. Drop the goal of forcing sleep and aim for quiet rest.
  2. If you feel stuck for around 20 minutes, leave bed briefly.
  3. Do one low-light, low-stimulation activity.
  4. Return to bed only when sleepiness picks back up.

I wake up in the middle of the night

  1. Check the clock as little as possible.
  2. Keep the bed from turning into a frustration zone.
  3. If you are wide awake, do a short quiet reset outside bed.
  4. Use slow breathing or a simple body scan, not sleep pressure.

My sleep schedule is broken

  1. Pick one realistic wake time and protect it first.
  2. Track your actual sleep for at least several days.
  3. Avoid chasing sleep with long naps or random catch-up mornings.
  4. Shift bedtime gradually after your wake time becomes steadier.
